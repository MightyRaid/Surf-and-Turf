The Recipe Cook

Start the potatoes first, lobster next, steak last — everything rests and lands on the plate together.

Pan-Seared Ribeye with Herb Butter

2 adult portions + 1 kid portion, cut into bites
Cut across the grain
  1. Take steaks out of the fridge 30–40 minutes before cooking so they cook more evenly. Trim off any large hard pieces of exterior fat, but leave the marbling alone.
  2. Pat completely dry with paper towel — moisture on the surface stops it from browning properly — and season generously with salt on both sides.
  3. Heat a heavy pan (your HexClad skillet is perfect) over high heat until just smoking. Add a high-smoke-point oil.
  4. Sear steak 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare, pressing down lightly for even contact. Use tongs to also sear the fat edge, standing the steak up, for about 30 seconds.
  5. In the last minute, add butter, smashed garlic cloves and thyme/rosemary. Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak continuously.
  6. Rest steak on a board for 5–8 minutes before slicing against the grain (cutting across the muscle fibers, not parallel to them) into finger-width strips.
  7. For the kid's portion: cook a smaller piece a little longer (medium/well), rest it the same way, then cut first into strips against the grain, and cut those strips again into small pea-to-thumbnail-sized cubes — small enough for a 5-year-old to chew easily.

Garlic Butter Lobster Tail

2–3 tails, depending on size
Split shell, lift meat out
  1. If frozen, thaw tails in the fridge overnight, or in a bowl of cold water for 30–45 minutes if you're short on time. Pat completely dry with paper towel once thawed.
  2. Butterfly the shell: using kitchen shears, cut straight down the center of the top (hard, curved) shell, starting from where the tail meets the body down to just before the tail fan. Cut through the shell only, not the meat underneath.
  3. Use your fingers to gently spread the cut shell apart. Run your thumb underneath the meat to loosen it from the shell on both sides, keeping the meat attached at the base near the tail fan.
  4. Lift the meat up and out through the split shell, then rest it on top of the shell, letting the shell halves close underneath like a little boat. This is what makes it easy to poach and elegant to plate.
  5. Devein if needed: run the tip of a knife shallowly down the top center of the meat — if you see a thin dark vein, lift it out with the knife tip or your fingers and discard. Small/farmed tails often won't have a visible one.
  6. In a small saucepan over low heat, melt butter with a splash of water and 1–2 minced garlic cloves, whisking so it doesn't split, until fragrant and silky.
  7. Lower lobster meat into the warm (not boiling) garlic butter and poach gently for 4–6 minutes until just opaque and firm.
  8. Remove, season lightly with salt, and finish with a squeeze of lemon and a spoon of the garlic butter just before serving.
  9. For the kid's portion: serve a small piece plain with plain butter (set some aside before adding garlic), cut into small pieces.

French Green Beans (Haricots Verts)

Side for the table
Trim the stem ends
  1. Trim the beans: line up a handful at a time and cut off the tough stem ends with one slice. The thin tail end is tender and fine to leave on.
  2. Blanch beans in well-salted boiling water for 2–3 minutes until bright green and just tender. Shock in ice water to stop cooking, then drain well.
  3. In a pan, melt butter and gently cook sliced shallot and minced garlic until fragrant, not browned.
  4. Toss in the beans and sauté 2–3 minutes to warm through and coat in the garlic butter.
  5. Finish with toasted sliced almonds and a pinch of flaky salt.

Creamy Parmesan Mash

Side for the table — no garlic, no chives
Even 1.5–2" cubes
  1. Choose the right potato: starchy or all-purpose potatoes mash best — russet/baking potatoes or Yukon Gold. Avoid waxy potatoes (like small red or new potatoes), they turn gluey when mashed.
  2. Peel with a vegetable peeler, going all the way around until no skin remains. Cut out any eyes or blemishes with the tip of a knife.
  3. Cut into even chunks, roughly 1.5–2 inch (4–5cm) cubes. Keeping the pieces the same size means they all finish cooking at the same time — uneven pieces mean some go mushy while others are still hard.
  4. Place the cut potatoes in a pot and cover with cold water by about an inch, add a generous pinch of salt. Starting from cold water (not adding potatoes to already-boiling water) cooks them more evenly through.
  5. Bring to a boil, then drop to a simmer and cook until fork-tender, about 15–18 minutes. You'll know they're ready when a knife slides in with no resistance.
  6. Drain well and pass through a ricer or mash thoroughly while hot.
  7. Fold in warm butter and warm milk/cream gradually until smooth, light and extra creamy.
  8. Stir in grated parmesan until fully melted through, then season with salt to taste.
Other options if you want to switch it up: a classic buttermilk mash (swap the cream for buttermilk, extra tangy), or fold in a spoon of cream cheese for an even richer, silkier texture. Both stay garlic and chive-free.
For the birthday girl (age 5): plate her steak bites and lobster pieces separately, unseasoned beyond a little salt, with extra mashed potato — no pepper, no lemon, nothing spicy.

The Game Plan Timeline

Built around 2 big burners + 1 small burner, your oven for holding things warm, and the HexClad set. Times count down to serving.

T−60 min
OvenPrep

Take steaks out of the fridge to come to room temperature. Preheat the oven to 90°C (200°F) — you'll use it to hold cooked food warm, not to cook in.

T−45 min
Big Burner 1HexClad Pot

Start the potatoes: large HexClad pot of salted water on your first big burner. Bring to a boil, then simmer.

T−35 min
Big Burner 2HexClad Pot

While potatoes simmer, blanch the green beans in a second pot on your other big burner — 2–3 minutes, then straight into ice water to stop cooking. Set aside.

T−27 min
Big Burner 1Oven

Potatoes should be fork-tender — drain, mash, fold in butter, cream and parmesan. Cover and park it in the oven to stay warm.

T−20 min
Prep

Shell the lobster tails, resting the meat on top of the shell. Mince the garlic and shallot you'll need for the lobster and the beans.

T−15 min
Small BurnerSmall Saucier

Melt butter and garlic gently in your small saucier on the small burner. Set aside a little plain butter for your daughter's portion first, then poach the lobster meat 4–6 minutes on low heat. Once done, cover loosely off the heat — it'll stay warm on residual heat.

T−10 min
Big Burner 1HexClad Skillet

Burner 1 is free now — get it ripping hot with a HexClad skillet. Sear the steaks 3–4 minutes a side, basting with herb butter in the last minute.

T−4 min
RestingBig Burner 2

Move steak to a board to rest. While it rests, use Burner 2 to quickly sauté the blanched beans in garlic butter, 2–3 minutes, finishing with toasted almonds.

T−0
Plate

Slice the rested steak, plate the lobster with a spoon of garlic butter over it, add the beans and a generous scoop of parmesan mash. Serve immediately.
